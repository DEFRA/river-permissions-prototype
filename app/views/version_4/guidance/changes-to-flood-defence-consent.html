How the Environmental Permitting Regulations affect existing Flood Defence Consents and applications already submitted.

On 6 April 2016 any activity with a Flood Defence Consent (FDC) came under environmental permitting rules. Check which of the following categories your activity comes under:

  • activities covered by environmental permitting
  • exemptions
  • exclusions
  • activities not covered by environmental permitting

Activities covered by environmental permitting

Your FDC will automatically become an environmental permit if it’s for activities that need an environmental permit.

You must continue to carry out the activity within the conditions of your FDC.

If you haven’t completed the work your FDC covers and want to change the details of the permit, you must apply to change the permit.

Subsistence charges will apply where your FDC becomes an environmental permit.

If your activity is exempt

You don’t need to apply for an exemption - your FDC automatically becomes an exemption. Read the exempt flood risk activities to find out:

  • if your activity is exempt
  • the conditions that apply to your work if it’s an exempt activity

You can be prosecuted if you don’t operate within the conditions of your exemption.

If your activity is an exclusion

Read the excluded flood risk activities to find out:

  • if your activity is excluded
  • the conditions you must carry out your work under if it’s an excluded activity

You can be prosecuted if you don’t operate within the conditions of your exclusion.

Activities not covered by environmental permitting

If your FDC isn’t one of the activities that need an environmental permit, your FDC will lapse. You can continue to do your activity and must comply with any other environmental legislation that may apply to your activity.[

If you don’t follow the conditions of your permit

Find out how the Environment Agency will regulate you if you do activities not covered by your permit or you break the rules of your permit after 6 April 2016.

Ongoing FDC applications

If you applied for an FDC but didn’t receive it before 6 April 2016, the Environment Agency will treat it as an application for an environmental permit.

The Environment Agency will return your application to you if your activity doesn’t need an environmental permit.
