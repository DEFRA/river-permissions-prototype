Outfalls and culverts: check if you need a permit to do work

First check if the outfall or culvert is on a main river

You will usually need permission to do work under or near a main river or flood defence that could affect flood risk, land drainage or access to the river. You don’t need permission to work on ‘ordinary watercourses’ such as small rivers, streams and ditches. But you may need land drainage consent.

How to check if your work is on a main river

Building a new outfall

Up to 300mm diameter

If you can meet the conditions you can register an exemption to build an outfall up to 300mm diameter. If you can't meet the conditions you will need to apply for a permit.

300mm to 500mm diameter

If you can build the outfall following our standard rules for outfalls between 300mm and 500mm diameter you could apply for a standard rules permit.

Over 500mm diameter

You will need a bespoke permit for an outfall over 500mm diameter.

Building a new culvert

If the culvert is up to 5 metres long and you can build it following our standard rules, you could apply for a standard rules permit.

If the culvert is longer than 5 metres, you will need a bespoke permit.

Repairs or maintenance to any outfalls or culverts

If you are removing any material from an existing culvert you can register an exemption if you meet the conditions.

Minor works to roads and rights of way over culverts

You don’t need a permit or to register to do minor works to roads and rights of way over culverts.

Demolishing outfalls or culverts

You will need a bespoke permit to demolish an outfall or culvert.

Emergency work

You don't need a permit for activities carried out in an emergency.

An emergency is a situation that presents a risk of serious flooding, serious damage to land drainage or serious harm to the environment. You must write to the Environment Agency as soon as you can afterwards, to tell them what work you have done and the circumstances of the emergency.
