Environmental management – guidance

Register your flood risk activity exemption: environmental permits

From:
Environment Agency and Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs
First published:
6 April 2016
Part of:
Flooding and coastal change, Environmental permits and Flooding and coastal change
Applies to:
England

How and when you can register for an exemption to do work near a main river or flood or sea defence without a permit.

Contents

  1. When you can’t get an exemption
  2. Register your exemption

You can only operate without a permit if your activity is listed as one of the activities exempt from environmental permitting.

You must register your exemption before you start work. You can only register as exempt if your activity:

  • meets the description and conditions of your exemption
  • doesn’t increase flood risk, adversely affect land drainage or harm the environment

If you don’t follow these rules and you continue to work without an environmental permit, your work could be illegal and the Environment Agency may take enforcement action or prosecute you.

If you can’t meet the description and conditions of an exemption, you must apply for an environmental permit.

When you can’t get an exemption

You won’t be able to register an exemption to carry out work at certain locations if they are:

  • environmentally sensitive, eg a European protected site or a Site of Special Scientific Interest
  • too close to a flood defence structure operated by the Environment Agency

Register your exemption

It’s free to register an exemption. If you want to register more than one exemption you must complete a separate registration for each.

Register your flood risk activity exemptions online. It should take about 10 minutes.

You’ll need:

  • the description for the exemption you want to register - you can find these in the exempt flood risk activities guidance
  • name and address of the individual, business or organisation that will be carrying out the activity
  • registration number and registered address details if you’re registering the activity for a limited company or limited liability partnership
  • name and address of the person who will be sent the registration confirmation
  • a 12-digit National Grid reference for the location of your work - you can use the free Ordnance Survey online map (opens new window) to find this.

If you register on behalf of an organisation, or anther person, check you have authority to do that.
