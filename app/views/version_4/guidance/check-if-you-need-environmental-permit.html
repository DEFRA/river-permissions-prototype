Environmental management – guidance

Check if you need an environmental permit

From:
Environment Agency and Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs
First published:
6 April 2016
Part of:
Flooding and coastal change, Environmental permits and Flooding and coastal change
Applies to:
England

Find out if you need a permit for works that cause pollution, increase flood risk or affect drainage.

Contents

  1. What you need a permit for
  2. Who to apply to
  3. Contact the Environment Agency

You may need an environmental permit for:

  • installations
  • waste operations
  • water discharge or groundwater activities
  • work on or near a main river or sea defence

You can be prosecuted if you operate without getting the permit you need. If you have a flood defence consent (FDC) or have applied for one, check how you could be affected by law changes on 6 April 2016.

What you need a permit for

You need an environmental permit to operate or carry out any of the following:

  • an ‘installation’ – a business that produces potentially harmful substances
  • a waste facility – where you recycle, store, treat or dispose of waste
  • a mining waste facility – which manages waste produced from mines or quarries
  • a small waste incineration plant – where you burn waste
  • mobile plant – machinery you can wheel or drive between locations
  • activity using solvents – releasing organic solvents directly or indirectly into the air
  • a stand-alone water discharge activity – releasing pollution to rivers, streams or the sea
  • a stand-alone groundwater activity – releasing polluting liquids directly or indirectly to water underground
  • activity that uses radioactive substances

You may also need an environmental permit for flood risk activities if you do work:

  • in, under, over or near a main river (including where the river is in a culvert)
  • on or near a flood defence on a main river
  • in the floodplain of a main river
  • on or near a sea defence

Who to apply to

Depending on your activity and the pollution risk, you need to apply to the Environment Agency or your local council.

Check the table to see who you need to apply to. If it’s the Environment Agency, there’s guidance for that activity, including whether there are:

  • exclusions (when you won’t need a permit)
  • exemptions (when you won’t need a permit but you may need to register)
What you do Who to apply to
Installation Check the guidance on the regulations to find out. If your activity is listed as ‘Part A1’, apply to the Environment Agency. If it’s ‘Part A2’ or ‘Part B’, apply to your local council
Waste or mining waste operation Environment Agency – read the guidance on how to apply
Small waste incineration plant Check the guidance to find out
Mobile plant Environment Agency if the plant deals with waste – read the guidance on how to apply, otherwise apply to your local council
Solvent emissions activity Your local council
Stand-alone water discharge or groundwater activity Environment Agency – read the guidance on how to apply
Flood risk activities on or near a main river or sea defence Environment Agency – read the guidance on how to apply
Radioactive substances Environment Agency – read the guidance on how to apply for nuclear sites or non-nuclear sites

 

If you’re not sure who to apply to, contact the Environment Agency. If you have a query about a flood risk activity, you will need to provide the location and postcode (or National Grid reference) of the site where you will carry out the work.
