Conditions for FRA3
Service crossing below the bed of a main river by directional drilling

Your activity must meet all of these conditions to be exempt. This is a guide and not the legal document.

Design of the works

The crossing is:

  • at right angles (+/- 10 degrees) to the direction of flow
  • at least 1.5m under the river bed
  • drilled level from 5 metres each side of the bank
  • built so the launch and reception pits beyond the bank is at least:
    • 8 metres for a non-tidal river
    • 16 metres for a tidal river
  • not passing through any bank, culvert, remote defence, river control works or sea defence
  • 50 metres or more upstream of any impoundment or artificially raised channel

How you carry out the work

  • you must erect permanent hazard markers on both banks
  • you must remove all excavated material from the flood plain unles it is re-used
  • your works must not disturb the bed and banks of the river

Location of the work

  • you cannot carry out the activity on, or within 200m of, a site designated as a Special Protection Area (SPA), Special Area for Conservation (SAC), Ramsar site, Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI) or Local Nature Reserve (LNR)
  • you cannot carry out any work in or within 100m of a river or lake in England or (if your activity is near the border) in Wales that is classified as of high morphological status under the Water Framework Directive
