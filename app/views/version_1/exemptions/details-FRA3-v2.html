Conditions for FRA3
Service crossing under a river by directional drilling

Your activity must meet all these conditions to be exempt. This is a guide and not the legal document.

Design of the work

The crossing is:

  • at right angles (+/- 10 degrees) to the direction of flow
  • at least 1.5m below the river bed
  • drilled level from 5m each side of the bank
  • built so the launch and reception pits are at least:
    • 8m beyond the bank for a non-tidal river
    • 16m beyond the bank for a tidal river
  • not passing through any bank, culvert, remote defence, river control works or sea defence
  • 50m or more upstream of any impoundment or artificially raised channel

How you carry out the work

You must:

  • erect permanent hazard markers on both banks
  • remove all excavated material from the flood plain unless it is re-used
  • not allow the works to disturb the bed or banks

Location of the work

The work cannot be on or within:

  • 200m of a:
    • Special Protection Area
    • Special Area for Conservation
    • Ramsar site
    • Site of Special Scientific Interest
    • Local Nature Reserve
  • 100m of a river or lake that is classified as of high morphological status
